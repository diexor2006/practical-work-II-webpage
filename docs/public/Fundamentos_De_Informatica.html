    FCE

    Description of the Subject

    The primary goal of the "Fundamentals of Computer Engineering" course is to give students a thorough grasp of computer engineering from a worldwide standpoint, covering both academic and professional aspects. The goal of this course is to give students a foundational understanding of subjects including physics, mathematics, anthropology, and ethics. Furthermore, it seeks to clarify the crucial role that computer engineers play in modern society as well as potential career paths in the industry. For this course, students must commit 150 working hours in total.

    There are two separate parts to the course:To put it another way,

    • Foundations in theory.To put it another way,

    • An introduction to web development in Practical Foundations.

    • To impart information on data management, computing trends, contemporary paradigms, and an overview of web programming.

    • To comprehend the ultimate objective of computer engineering, its purpose, and the benefits it offers society, while considering the part each person hopes to play as an engineer in the society of the future.

    • To learn about the foundations that technical advancement has to be built upon, including a historical viewpoint on technological growth.

    • To encourage a sense of service and contribution to the common good and acknowledge the significance of the human element in professional work.

    • To question one's own vocation by having a broad perspective on the subject of study, its many knowledge domains, and the ability to differentiate between professional profiles and career trajectories.

    • To provide knowledge about current paradigms, trends in computing, data management, and an introduction to web development.

    • To impart information on data management, computing trends, contemporary paradigms, and an overview of web programming.
